Recommendation Memo

Executive Summary

The new onboarding and activation campaign improved the main success metric, paid conversion rate. Treatment users converted at 7.04%, compared with 3.19% for Control users. The improvement is statistically significant with a p-value of 0.0005.

North Star Metric

The North Star metric is Paid Conversion Rate because the goal is to convert more users into paid subscribers.

KPI Tree Explanation

Paid conversion depends on acquisition quality, activation quality, engagement quality, and revenue quality. Supporting metrics explain the funnel movement, while guardrail metrics protect the business from poor-quality growth.

Experiment Result Summary

Treatment improved paid conversion rate by 3.85% absolute points. Treatment also improved onboarding completion, engagement score, and average days to convert.

Hypothesis Test Interpretation

A one-tailed two-proportion z-test was used. Since p-value = 0.0005, which is below 0.05, the conversion improvement is statistically significant.

Guardrail Analysis

Guardrails reviewed include refund rate, support ticket rate, average engagement score, average days to convert, and revenue quality. Treatment had better engagement and faster conversion, but support tickets and refunds should be monitored after launch.

Segment-Level Insight

Treatment improved paid conversion across most major segments. The segment analysis in outputs/experiment_summary.xlsx should be used to monitor rollout performance by region, device type, traffic source, and plan type.

Final Recommendation

Launch with monitoring.

The campaign should be launched because it produces a statistically significant improvement in paid conversion rate. However, the rollout should include monitoring for support tickets, refund rate, and revenue quality.

Risks and Limitations


30-day revenue may not represent long-term customer value.
Refund behavior may increase after a wider rollout.
Segment-level results may vary due to smaller sample sizes.
Duplicate user IDs were removed before final analysis.
