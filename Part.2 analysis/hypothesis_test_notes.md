Hypothesis Test Notes

Metric Being Tested

Paid Conversion Rate

Paid Conversion Rate = Users converted to paid subscription / Total users

Reason for Choosing This Metric

Paid conversion rate is the North Star metric because the business decision is whether the new onboarding campaign should be launched. The campaign is successful only if it increases the percentage of users who become paying subscribers.

Null Hypothesis

H0: The Treatment group does not have a higher paid conversion rate than the Control group.

Alternative Hypothesis

H1: The Treatment group has a higher paid conversion rate than the Control group.

Test Type

One-tailed two-proportion z-test.

This is one-tailed because the business question is whether Treatment improves conversion compared with Control.

Significance Level

Alpha = 0.05

Test Inputs

Test Output

Decision Rule

If p-value < 0.05, reject H0.

Result

The p-value is 0.0005, which is less than 0.05. Therefore, reject H0.

Business Interpretation

The Treatment group shows a statistically significant improvement in paid conversion rate. This supports launching the new onboarding campaign, provided guardrail metrics continue to be monitored.
